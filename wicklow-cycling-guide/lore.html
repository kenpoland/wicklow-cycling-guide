🧚 Wicklow’s Enchanted Tales

🕊️ Glendalough – The Blackbird & The Stone

St. Kevin was said to have held out his hand in prayer so long that a blackbird nested in it and hatched her chicks. Nearby, a stone bowl called the Deer Stone still “fills with milk” to feed orphaned children. Magic or miracle?

💧 Glenmacnass Waterfall – Spirits in the Mist

Rebels once hid here during the 1798 uprising. Local legend claims the mists carry their voices, and that nature spirits dance by the waterfall at dusk.

🍺 Lough Tay – Portal to the Otherworld

Its still waters and crescent-shaped shoreline resemble a pint of Guinness. But some call it a fairy lake, where timeless music drifts across the hills under moonlight.

🏰 Castle Kevin (Annamoe) – Betrayed & Bound

Only ruins remain, but villagers whisper that a betrayed constable haunts the castle ruins. On midsummer nights, fairy lights flicker along the riverside like distant lanterns.

🐕 Roundwood – The Watchful Giant

They say a giant sheepdog once protected Roundwood from raiders, so fierce its spirit still lingers in the fog. Some riders claim to hear distant paws on misty trails.
